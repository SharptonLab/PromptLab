- Comparison: high-fat diet vs control diet
- Directionality: positive log2FoldChange means higher expression in high-fat diet compared with control
- Main biological interests: hepatic lipid metabolism, steatosis, inflammation, insulin resistance, oxidative stress, fibrosis, and mitochondrial/metabolic remodeling

Input data:
I will provide a table with the following columns, if available:
- gene_id
- gene_symbol
- baseMean or mean expression
- log2FoldChange
- pvalue
- padj/FDR

Analysis criteria:
1. Prioritize genes with adjusted p-value/FDR < 0.05.
2. Among significant genes, prioritize larger absolute log2 fold changes.
3. Treat genes with low expression cautiously, even if fold change is large.
4. Separate upregulated genes from downregulated genes.
5. Do not claim a gene is important unless the provided data supports it.
6. If relevant information is missing, state what is missing rather than guessing.

Please provide the output in the following format:

## Summary
Briefly summarize the overall differential expression pattern, including the number of significant upregulated and downregulated genes if this can be determined.

## Top upregulated genes in high-fat diet
Provide a table with:
- Rank
- Gene symbol
- log2FoldChange
- adjusted p-value/FDR
- Brief biological relevance to mouse liver or high-fat diet biology
- Reason for prioritization

## Top downregulated genes in high-fat diet
Provide the same table for downregulated genes.

## Key biological themes
Identify major pathways or processes suggested by the gene list, such as:
- Lipid metabolism
- Inflammation/immune response
- Fibrosis/extracellular matrix remodeling
- Oxidative stress
- Mitochondrial function
- Bile acid metabolism

- Strong statistical support
- Meaningful effect size
- Clear relevance to the biological question
- Mix of upregulated and downregulated genes, if appropriate

- Volcano plot
