### Critique of the Original Prompt

**Prompt:** `Look at my RNA-seq results and tell me which genes are important. The data is from a mouse liver study comparing high-fat diet to control.`

#### 1. What's ambiguous or underspecified?

*   **"Look at my RNA-seq results"**: The prompt doesn't specify the format of the results. Is it a CSV file? A list of gene names? A full output table from a tool like DESeq2 or edgeR? The model needs to know what columns are available (e.g., `gene_id`, `log2FoldChange`, `pvalue`, `padj`/`FDR`).
*   **"tell me which genes are important"**: This is the most critical ambiguity. "Important" is subjective and needs to be defined quantitatively.
    *   Does "important" mean **statistically significant**? If so, based on what metric (p-value, adjusted p-value) and what threshold (e.g., p < 0.05)?
    *   Does it mean **biologically significant**? This usually implies a large magnitude of change. What is the log2 Fold Change (log2FC) threshold (e.g., |log2FC| > 1)?
    *   Does it mean the **most-discussed in the literature** for this condition?
    *   Does it mean the ones with the **highest expression levels** (base mean)?
    *   The prompt forces the AI to guess the user's definition of "important."

#### 2. What assumptions am I making that might not hold?

*   **Assumption of a single definition of "important"**: You are assuming the AI shares your implicit definition of what makes a gene "important" in a DGE analysis. A bioinformatician would immediately ask for your p-value and fold-change cutoffs.
*   **Assumption of context-awareness**: You assume the AI will automatically know *why* you're asking. Is your goal to find drug targets? To understand the basic mechanism of fatty liver disease? To find biomarkers? The definition of "important" can change depending on the goal.
*   **Assumption of data parsing**: You assume the AI can correctly interpret any table you paste, correctly identifying the columns for gene names, fold change, and statistical significance without explicit instruction.

#### 3. What failure modes might this prompt produce?

*   **Generic, Unhelpful Output**: The AI might respond with, "Important genes are typically those with a low adjusted p-value and a high fold change. Please provide your data and specify your thresholds." This doesn't perform the analysis and puts the work back on you.
*   **Hallucination/Incorrect Guessing**: The AI could pick arbitrary thresholds (e.g., padj < 0.05, |log2FC| > 2) that don't match your study's needs. It might produce a list of genes that isn't what you were looking for.
*   **Focusing on the Wrong Metric**: The AI might seize on only one metric. For example, it could list the genes with the tiniest p-values, even if their fold changes are biologically negligible (e.g., log2FC = 0.1). Or, it could list genes with massive fold changes that are not statistically significant.
*   **Narrative without Data**: The AI might ignore the data you provide and instead give a summary of canonical genes known to be involved in high-fat diet-induced liver disease (e.g., *Srebf1*, *Ppara*, *Fasn*), whether they are significant in your specific dataset or not.

#### 4. How would you improve it to make outputs more consistent and reliable?

To improve the prompt, you need to be **explicit, structured, and specific**.

1.  **Set the Persona**: Tell the AI to act as an expert (e.g., "Act as a bioinformatician").
2.  **Define the Goal**: State the overall objective of your analysis.
3.  **Define Key Terms**: Clearly define what "important" or "significant" means by providing quantitative thresholds (e.g., `padj < 0.05`, `|log2FC| > 1`).
4.  **Specify the Input**: Describe the data you will provide, including the names of the critical columns.
5.  **Structure the Output**: Tell the AI exactly what you want it to produce and in what format (e.g., a summary, markdown tables, a biological interpretation). This is the most powerful way to control the output.

### Revised and Improved Prompt

Here is a revised version that incorporates these principles. It turns a vague question into a precise, multi-step analytical task.

**Version 1: Standard & Effective**

> Act as a bioinformatician analyzing differential gene expression data. My goal is to identify and interpret the most significant differentially expressed genes (DEGs) from an RNA-seq experiment.
>
> **Experiment Context:** The data is from mouse liver tissue, comparing a high-fat diet group to a control group.
>
> **Analysis Instructions:**
>
> 1.  **Define Significance:** A gene is considered significant if it has an adjusted p-value (FDR) < 0.05 AND an absolute log2 fold change > 1.
>
> 2.  **Input Data:** I will paste a table of results. The key columns are `gene_symbol`, `log2FoldChange`, and `padj`.
>
> 3.  **Your Tasks:**
>     a. **Summary:** First, calculate and report the total number of significant DEGs, broken down into up-regulated and down-regulated genes based on my criteria.
>     b. **Top Gene Tables:** Create two markdown tables:
>         - The top 10 most significant up-regulated genes.
>         - The top 10 most significant down-regulated genes.
>         - **Sort both tables by adjusted p-value (ascending).** Each table must include the `gene_symbol`, `log2FoldChange`, and `padj` columns.
>     c. **Biological Interpretation:** For the top 3 genes from *each* table, provide a brief (1-2 sentence) summary of their known function, especially in the context of liver metabolism, lipid synthesis, or inflammation.
>
> Please confirm you understand these instructions, and I will provide the data.

**Why this is better:**

*   **Unambiguous:** "Important" is now precisely defined as `padj < 0.05` and `|log2FC| > 1`.
*   **Structured:** It requests a specific, multi-part output (a summary, two tables, and an interpretation), which prevents generic answers.
*   **Contextualized:** It tells the AI not just what the experiment is, but also what biological themes to focus on for the interpretation (metabolism, inflammation).
*   **Reliable:** By specifying the input columns (`gene_symbol`, `log2FoldChange`, `padj`) and the sorting criteria for the output tables, you ensure the results are consistent and correctly prioritized every time.
*   **Interactive Checkpoint:** The final sentence ("Please confirm...") ensures the AI has processed the instructions before you send the data, reducing the chance of it ignoring your detailed commands.
