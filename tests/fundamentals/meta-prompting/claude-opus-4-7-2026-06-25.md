# Critique of Your Prompt

## 1. What's Ambiguous or Underspecified

- **"Look at my RNA-seq results"** — What data are you actually providing? A DESeq2/edgeR output table? Raw counts? A volcano plot? The model can't analyze data you don't share.
- **"Important"** — Important how? Statistically significant? Largest fold change? Biologically relevant to lipid metabolism? Druggable? Novel?
- **No thresholds specified** — What adjusted p-value cutoff? What log2FC threshold? Are you filtering by baseMean/expression level?
- **No output format** — Ranked list? Table? Pathway-grouped? How many genes?
- **No biological context** — Which liver cells? Diet duration? Mouse strain? Sex? These dramatically affect interpretation.
- **No prior knowledge specified** — Should the model focus on known NAFLD/obesity genes, or flag novel findings?

## 2. Hidden Assumptions

- That the model can infer "importance" without your specific scientific question
- That standard significance thresholds (padj < 0.05, |log2FC| > 1) are what you want
- That the model has reliable knowledge of every gene's function (it will confabulate for less-studied genes)
- That gene symbols in your data are unambiguous (mouse vs. human conventions, aliases)
- That differential expression equals biological importance (it often doesn't)

## 3. Likely Failure Modes

- **Hallucinated gene functions** — especially for less-characterized genes
- **Generic answers** — model lists obvious metabolism genes (Srebf1, Ppara, Fasn) regardless of what's actually in your data
- **Inconsistent outputs** — different runs emphasize different genes
- **No uncertainty calibration** — confident assertions about gene roles that may be context-specific
- **Missing the actual signal** — without your effect sizes and p-values, ranking is arbitrary
- **Ignoring multiple testing or batch effects** that should inform interpretation

## 4. Improvements

- Provide the actual data (or a structured subset)
- Define "important" operationally
- Specify thresholds, output format, and length
- Give biological context and your hypothesis
- Ask for uncertainty flags and citations where possible
- Request the model distinguish well-established vs. speculative interpretations

# Revised Prompt

```
ROLE: Act as a computational biologist helping me interpret 
differential expression results.

DATA: I'm attaching a DESeq2 output table with columns: 
gene_symbol, ensembl_id, baseMean, log2FoldChange, lfcSE, 
stat, pvalue, padj. [PASTE DATA OR ATTACH FILE]

EXPERIMENTAL CONTEXT:
- Species/tissue: Mus musculus, whole liver, bulk RNA-seq
- Strain/sex/age: C57BL/6J, male, 12 weeks at start
- Conditions: 60% high-fat diet vs. standard chow, 16 weeks
- Replicates: n=6 per group
- Comparison direction: HFD vs. control (positive log2FC = up in HFD)

TASK: Identify and interpret the most biologically meaningful 
differentially expressed genes.

DEFINE "IMPORTANT" AS (apply all):
- padj < 0.05
- |log2FoldChange| >= 1
- baseMean >= 50 (exclude low-expression noise)

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Summary table of top 20 up- and top 20 down-regulated genes 
   passing thresholds, sorted by padj, with columns: 
   gene | log2FC | padj | known function (1 line) | confidence (high/medium/low)
2. Pathway-level grouping: cluster the significant genes into 
   functional categories (e.g., lipid metabolism, inflammation, 
   fibrosis, circadian, ER stress)
3. Highlight 3-5 genes most relevant to NAFLD/NASH biology and 
   explain why
4. Flag any genes whose function you are uncertain about — do 
   not fabricate functions
5. Note any unexpected findings or genes that contradict the 
   expected HFD signature

CONSTRAINTS:
- Mark speculative interpretations explicitly as "hypothesis"
- If a gene symbol is ambiguous, state both possibilities
- Do not invent citations; if you reference a paper, say 
  "likely reported in [context]" rather than fabricating a DOI
- If the data do not support a confident answer, say so

MY HYPOTHESIS (for context, not to bias): I expect upregulation 
of de novo lipogenesis and inflammation pathways; I'm particularly 
interested in genes that might explain inter-individual variation.
```
