# Critique of Your Prompt

## 1. What's Ambiguous or Underspecified

**"My RNA-seq results"** — The model has no idea what format the data is in (DESeq2 output? edgeR? a CSV? specific columns?). It can't actually analyze data it can't see.

**"Important"** — This is the biggest problem. Important by what criterion?
- Statistical significance (adjusted p-value threshold)?
- Effect size (log2 fold change threshold)?
- Biological relevance to lipid metabolism / liver function?
- Novelty (genes not previously linked to diet-induced hepatic changes)?
- Druggability or therapeutic potential?

**"Tell me"** — In what form? A ranked list? A table? Pathway analysis? A narrative summary? How many genes?

**Study context is thin** — No information about:
- Sample sizes (n per group)
- Diet duration and composition
- Mouse strain, sex, age
- Tissue prep / sequencing platform
- Whether you've already done QC and normalization

**No mention of what you'll do with the answer** — Hypothesis generation? Selecting candidates for qPCR validation? Writing a paper? This changes what's useful.

## 2. Hidden Assumptions

- That the model can "look at" data you haven't provided
- That there's a universal definition of "important genes" in RNA-seq
- That mouse liver + HFD is a context the model will correctly contextualize (it likely will lean on well-known genes like *Srebf1*, *Fasn*, *Cd36*, *Pparg* — which may or may not be what you want)
- That a single pass is enough (vs. iterative analysis)
- That you want gene-level results rather than pathway/module-level results

## 3. Likely Failure Modes

- **Confabulation**: The model may invent plausible-sounding gene lists ("Fasn was upregulated 4.2-fold") without any actual data — a serious risk in a scientific context.
- **Generic textbook answer**: You'll get a recital of well-known HFD-responsive liver genes regardless of what your data actually shows.
- **Wrong thresholds**: It may apply arbitrary cutoffs (|log2FC| > 1, padj < 0.05) without telling you, or use different ones than your field expects.
- **Missing the interesting biology**: By focusing on "important" canonical genes, it may overlook unexpected hits that are the actual scientific value of your experiment.
- **No uncertainty quantification**: No flagging of borderline calls, low-count genes, or batch effect concerns.

## 4. How to Improve It

- Actually provide the data (or a structured summary: top N by padj, with logFC, baseMean, etc.)
- Define "important" with explicit criteria
- Specify the output format
- State your downstream goal
- Ask the model to flag assumptions and caveats rather than power through them
- Request structured reasoning (e.g., separate sections for statistical hits, biological interpretation, and candidate prioritization)
