# Critique of Your RNA-seq Analysis Prompt

## 1. What's Ambiguous or Underspecified

**"Look at my RNA-seq results"**
- What format are the results in? (DESeq2 output, edgeR, limma-voom, raw counts, a CSV with specific columns?)
- Where are the results? (Pasted inline, file attachment, summary statistics?)
- What columns/fields are available? (log2FC, p-value, adjusted p-value, base mean, gene symbols, Ensembl IDs?)

**"Important"**
- Important by what criterion? Statistical significance? Effect size? Biological relevance? Druggability? Novelty?
- Important for what purpose? Hypothesis generation? Validation candidates? Pathway analysis? Publication figure?

**"Tell me which genes"**
- How many genes? Top 10? Top 50? All passing a threshold?
- In what format? Ranked list? Table? Grouped by function?

**Missing context**
- What thresholds define significance? (padj < 0.05? |log2FC| > 1?)
- Direction of interest: upregulated, downregulated, or both?
- Sample sizes, study design (timepoints, sex, age of mice)?
- Strain of mice, diet composition, duration?

## 2. Hidden Assumptions

- That the LLM can "see" your data (it can't unless you provide it)
- That "important" has a universally understood meaning in transcriptomics
- That standard thresholds are appropriate for your study
- That the LLM has up-to-date knowledge of liver-specific or NAFLD/NASH-related gene functions
- That gene symbols alone provide enough context (no species disambiguation needed)
- That the LLM should prioritize known genes over novel findings (or vice versa)

## 3. Likely Failure Modes

- **Hallucinated gene functions**: LLM may confidently describe gene roles incorrectly
- **Generic answers**: Returns well-known obesity/NAFLD genes (Srebf1, Pparg, Cd36) regardless of your actual data
- **No data grounding**: Without your actual results, the response will be based on prior knowledge of the field, not your experiment
- **Inconsistent rankings**: Different runs return different "important" gene lists
- **Missing statistical rigor**: May highlight genes with large fold changes but poor significance, or vice versa
- **No reproducibility**: No clear criteria means you can't validate or compare outputs

## 4. Improvements for Consistency and Reliability

- Provide the actual data (or a structured summary)
- Define explicit thresholds and ranking criteria
- Specify output format (table, columns, sort order)
- State the downstream purpose
- Ask for confidence/uncertainty flags
- Request the LLM to cite biological rationale rather than assert facts
- Separate "data filtering" from "biological interpretation" as distinct steps

ROLE: Act as a bioinformatician helping interpret differential expression results.

- Tissue: Liver

- Goal: Identify candidate genes for follow-up qPCR validation and pathway analysis

[Paste DESeq2 results table with columns: gene_symbol, ensembl_id, baseMean,

CRITERIA FOR "IMPORTANT" GENES (apply in this order):
1. Statistical significance: padj < 0.05
2. Effect size: |log2FoldChange| ≥ 1
3. Expression level: baseMean ≥ 50 (filter out low-count noise)
4. Biological relevance: prioritize genes with known roles in lipid metabolism, 
   insulin signaling, inflammation, fibrosis, or bile acid metabolism

TASK:
1. Filter the provided data using the criteria above. Report counts at each filter step.
2. Return the top 20 upregulated and top 20 downregulated genes meeting all criteria, 
   as a markdown table with columns: gene_symbol, log2FC, padj, baseMean, 
   known_function (1 sentence), confidence (high/medium/low based on how well-
   characterized the gene is).
3. Group these 40 genes into 3–5 functional themes with a 2-sentence rationale per theme.
4. Suggest 5 priority candidates for qPCR validation, with justification.

CONSTRAINTS:
- Do not invent gene functions. If you are uncertain, mark confidence as "low" 
  and say "function not well-characterized in liver" rather than guessing.
- Do not add genes that are not present in the provided data.
- If the data is missing required columns, stop and tell me what's missing.
- Distinguish clearly between (a) what the data shows and (b) biological 
  interpretation that requires literature follow-up.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
## Filtering Summary
## Top Upregulated Genes (table)
## Top Downregulated Genes (table)
## Functional Themes
## Priority Validation Candidates
## Caveats and Uncertainties
