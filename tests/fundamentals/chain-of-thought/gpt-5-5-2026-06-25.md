## Step 3 — Assumption evaluation for your specific data

### A. PERMANOVA

Potential issues:

1. **Repeated measures**
   - Standard PERMANOVA assumes independent samples unless the permutation scheme accounts for repeated measures.
   - Your 60 samples are not independent because they come from 20 mice.

2. **Cage effects**
   - If cage is ignored, treatment effects may be overstated.
   - If treatment is assigned by cage and there are few cages, treatment and cage may be partially confounded.

3. **Dispersion effects**
   - PERMANOVA can be significant if groups differ in variability rather than centroid location.
   - You should check beta-dispersion, e.g. with `betadisper` in R.

4. **Complex permutation design**
   - Correctly testing treatment, time, and treatment × time with both mouse and cage dependencies is not trivial.

PERMANOVA is useful, but I would treat it as a community-level omnibus test that needs careful implementation.
