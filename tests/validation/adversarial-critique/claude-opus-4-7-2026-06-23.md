# Adversarial Critique: Microbiome Methods Section

## Summary Assessment
This Methods section is **not reproducible as written**. A reviewer at any microbiome-focused journal (Microbiome, ISME J, Gut, mSystems) would almost certainly request major revisions. The phrase "appropriate methods" alone is a red flag that suggests insufficient methodological rigor. Below are specific issues.

---

## 1. Critical Reproducibility Failures

### "Standard protocols" — undefined
> *"Stool samples were collected from all participants using standard protocols."*

There is **no such thing as a single standard protocol**. You must specify:
- Collection device (e.g., OMNIgene-GUT, Fisherbrand commode specimen collector, home kit)
- Whether samples were collected at home or in clinic
- Time from defecation to freezing (critical — microbial composition shifts within hours at room temperature)
- Any stabilization buffer used
- Whether aliquoting occurred before freezing
- Storage duration before extraction

### "Immediately frozen" — ambiguous
What does "immediately" mean? Minutes? Hours? For home collection, were samples transported on dry ice? On wet ice? At ambient temperature? This single ambiguity can drive entire compositional differences between groups, especially if IBD patients collected samples differently than controls.

### "Default parameters" in QIIME2 — insufficient
> *"Sequences were processed using QIIME2 with default parameters"*

QIIME2 is a wrapper for many tools with different defaults. You must specify:
- QIIME2 version (results change between versions)
- Denoising method: DADA2 or Deblur? (these give different ASVs)
- Trimming/truncation parameters (e.g., `--p-trunc-len-f`, `--p-trunc-len-r`)
- Quality filtering thresholds
- Whether chimera removal was performed
- Rarefaction depth (critical for diversity metrics)

### "Appropriate methods" — meaningless
> *"All statistical analyses were performed using appropriate methods"*

This is the most problematic sentence in the paragraph. A reviewer will demand:
- Specific tests for alpha diversity (Wilcoxon? t-test? Were normality assumptions checked?)
- Specific tests for beta diversity (PERMANOVA? ANOSIM? How many permutations?)
- Differential abundance methods (ANCOM-BC, ALDEx2, MaAsLin2, DESeq2?)
- Multiple testing correction (BH-FDR? Bonferroni?)
- Software and version numbers

---

## 2. Substantive Methodological Concerns

### Greengenes is outdated
> *"taxonomy was assigned using the Greengenes database"*

Greengenes has not been substantively updated since **2013**. SILVA (current 138.2) or GTDB are now standard. A reviewer will ask why you chose an obsolete reference. If you used Greengenes2 (2022), say so explicitly — it's a different database.

### 515F/806R primer version not specified
There are multiple versions of these primers (Caporaso 2011 vs. Parada 2016 / Apprill 2015 — the latter improves coverage of Crenarchaeota and SAR11). Specify which.

### No mention of controls
For a case-control microbiome study, **omitting controls is a serious flaw**:
- Negative extraction controls (kit contamination is well-documented; see Salter et al. 2014)
- Negative PCR controls
- Positive mock community controls (e.g., Zymo)
- Without these, you cannot rule out batch effects or contamination driving group differences

### Batch effects not addressed
Were IBD and control samples extracted/sequenced in the same batches? If batches correlate with case status, your entire finding could be a batch artifact. This must be stated.

### No power justification for n=30/group
The Background mentions n=30 per group but the Methods don't justify this. For microbiome studies with high inter-individual variance, n=30 may be underpowered for detecting differential abundance of low-prevalence taxa.

---

## 3. Missing Considerations

- **Inclusion/exclusion criteria**: Antibiotic use within prior 3-6 months? PPI use? Diet? Bowel prep? All dramatically affect microbiome.
- **IBD subtype**: Crohn's vs. UC? Active vs. remission? Medications (biologics, steroids, immunomodulators)?
- **Matching**: Were controls age/sex/BMI-matched?
- **Sample storage duration**: Did IBD and control samples sit in -80°C for similar times?
- **Sequencing depth**: Target reads per sample? Minimum threshold for inclusion?
- **Read length and paired-end vs. single-end**

---

## 4. What a Skeptical Reviewer Would Write

> *"The Methods section as written does not permit reproduction of the analysis. The authors must specify QIIME2 version, denoising algorithm and parameters, rarefaction depth, and all statistical tests used. The use of Greengenes (last updated 2013) is inappropriate; reanalysis with SILVA 138 or Greengenes2 is required. Critically, the authors do not report negative controls for DNA extraction or PCR, nor do they address potential batch effects between case and control samples. Given the well-documented influence of collection methodology on microbiome composition (Vandeputte et al. 2017; Costea et al. 2017), the vague description of 'standard protocols' and 'immediate' freezing is inadequate. I cannot recommend publication without these methodological details and, ideally, reanalysis."*
