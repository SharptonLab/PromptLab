# Adversarial Critique: Microbiome Methods Section

## MAJOR CONCERNS

### 1. "Standard protocols" is meaningless
**Quote:** *"Stool samples were collected from all participants using standard protocols."*

There is no universal "standard protocol" for stool collection in microbiome research. This is one of the largest sources of variability in the field. Critical missing details:
- Self-collection vs. clinical collection?
- Collection device/kit (e.g., OMNIgene-GUT, sterile container, swab)?
- Time from defecation to freezing — was there a delay? Was it transported on ice/dry ice?
- Was the sample homogenized? Which part of the stool was sampled (microbial composition differs across a single stool)?
- Were collection conditions identical for IBD patients and controls? IBD patients often have diarrheal stool, which affects DNA yield and composition. **This is a major confound that must be addressed.**

### 2. "Immediately frozen at -80°C" is implausible
**Quote:** *"Samples were immediately frozen at -80°C"*

Unless participants defecated inside a freezer, this cannot be literally true. Reviewers will want:
- Time-to-freezer interval
- Intermediate storage (e.g., -20°C, dry ice, room-temperature preservative)
- Whether the interval differed between cohorts (a likely confounder if IBD patients were recruited from clinic and controls from community)

### 3. Missing IRB/ethics statement
No mention of ethical approval, informed consent, or IRB number. This is mandatory for human subjects research and will result in immediate rejection at most journals.

### 4. No participant characterization or exclusion criteria
For an IBD vs. control study, the methods must specify:
- IBD subtype (UC vs. CD), disease activity, location, medications (especially antibiotics, immunosuppressants, biologics — all of which affect the microbiome)
- Antibiotic use within 1–3 months (standard exclusion)
- Diet, BMI, age/sex matching
- PPI use, probiotic use
- Bowel prep prior to sampling?

Without these, the comparison is uninterpretable.

### 5. Inadequate sequencing/bioinformatics detail
**Quote:** *"QIIME2 with default parameters"*

Major omissions:
- QIIME2 version (results are not reproducible without this)
- Denoising method (DADA2? Deblur?) — these are not "default" choices
- Quality filtering parameters, truncation lengths
- Read depth, rarefaction depth, number of reads retained per sample
- Chimera removal
- Whether singletons were removed
- Negative/positive controls during extraction and sequencing (essential for low-biomass concerns and contamination)
- Batch information — were samples sequenced in one run or multiple? Were cases and controls balanced across runs?

### 6. Greengenes is outdated
**Quote:** *"taxonomy was assigned using the Greengenes database"*

Greengenes has not been meaningfully updated since 2013. Most current studies use SILVA (138+) or GTDB. Reviewers will ask why. Also: which version? What classifier (sklearn naive Bayes? VSEARCH)?

### 7. "Appropriate methods" is unacceptable
**Quote:** *"All statistical analyses were performed using appropriate methods"*

This is a non-statement. Required specifics:
- Alpha diversity comparison: parametric (t-test) or non-parametric (Wilcoxon)? Normality tested?
- Beta diversity: PERMANOVA? With how many permutations? Dispersion (PERMDISP) tested? Failing to test dispersion is a common error that invalidates PERMANOVA conclusions.
- Differential abundance: ANCOM-BC? ALDEx2? LEfSe? DESeq2? Each gives different results.
- Multiple testing correction (FDR/BH)?
- Covariate adjustment (age, sex, medications)?
- Software/package versions

### 8. Sample size justification absent
n=30/group is presented without power analysis. For microbiome studies, effect sizes vary enormously, and 30/group may be underpowered for differential abundance after FDR correction.

### 9. V4 region choice unjustified
The 515F/806R primers — which versions? The original Caporaso primers have known biases (e.g., against Bifidobacterium), and updated Parada/Apprill versions exist. Specify exact sequences.

## MINOR ISSUES

- "Manufacturer instructions" — note any deviations; PowerSoil has optional bead-beating steps that affect results
- No mention of read length (2x150? 2x250?) or sequencing depth target
- No data/code availability statement (often required in methods)
- p < 0.05 with no mention of correction for the many tests performed

## WHAT A SKEPTICAL REVIEWER WILL SAY

> "The methods as written are insufficient for reproducibility and raise serious concerns about confounding. The lack of detail regarding participant characteristics — particularly medication use and antibiotic exposure in the IBD cohort — makes any reported differences between groups uninterpretable as disease-associated rather than treatment-associated. The collection protocol is undescribed, and given known differences in stool consistency between IBD and healthy individuals, systematic collection/storage differences could entirely explain the findings. Bioinformatics parameters are missing, Greengenes is obsolete, and the statistical section is essentially absent. Major revision required, with the possibility that the underlying study design cannot support the intended comparisons."

## RECOMMENDED REWRITE PRIORITIES

1. Add ethics/consent paragraph
2. Add detailed inclusion/exclusion and clinical metadata collection
3. Specify exact collection, transport, and storage protocol with time intervals
4. Provide complete bioinformatics pipeline with versions and parameters
5. Replace Greengenes with SILVA or justify
6. Write a real statistics paragraph naming each test, package, and correction
7. Add controls (extraction blanks, mock communities) or justify their absence
8. Add power/sample size justification
