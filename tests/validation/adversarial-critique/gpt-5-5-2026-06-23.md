This Methods paragraph is too generic to be reproducible and would likely be criticized as insufficient for a microbiome study, especially one comparing IBD patients with healthy controls. The largest problems are: vague sample collection/storage procedures, missing control of major microbiome confounders, inadequate sequencing and bioinformatics detail, use of an outdated taxonomy database, unspecified statistical methods, and overreliance on phrases like “standard protocols,” “default parameters,” and “appropriate methods.”

# 1. Logical weaknesses

## Significant concern: “Standard protocols” is not a method

> “Stool samples were collected from all participants using standard protocols.”

This is essentially non-informative. “Standard protocols” vary widely across microbiome studies and can materially affect results. A reviewer cannot evaluate reproducibility or bias.

Missing details include:

- Was stool collected at home or in clinic?
- What collection kit was used?
- Was there a stabilizing buffer?
- How long elapsed between defecation and freezing?
- Were samples kept on ice, refrigerated, or at room temperature before freezing?
- Were participants given written instructions?
- Was collection time standardized, e.g., morning sample?
- Were samples homogenized before aliquoting?
- Were aliquots freeze-thawed?

**Recommended fix:** Replace “standard protocols” with a precise collection procedure, including collection kit, timing, transport, stabilization, and processing window.

---

## Significant concern: “Immediately frozen” may be implausible or misleading

> “Samples were immediately frozen at -80°C…”

This is suspiciously clean. If samples were collected by participants at home, immediate -80°C freezing is unlikely unless they had access to ultra-low freezers. If collected in clinic, the logistics should be stated. If “immediately” means within several hours, say that.

This matters because stool microbiome profiles can shift with temperature and time-to-freezing.

**Recommended fix:** State the actual median and range of time from collection to freezing, transport conditions, and whether this differed by group.

Example:

> “Participants collected stool at home using OMNIgene-GUT kits and returned samples within 24 h. Samples were aliquoted within 2 h of receipt and stored at −80°C. Time from collection to storage did not differ between groups.”

If no stabilization buffer was used, the authors need to justify sample integrity.

The study compares healthy controls and IBD patients, but the methods do not address major confounders that can drive microbiome differences independently of IBD.

Missing variables include:

- Recent antibiotic use

- Steroids, biologics, immunomodulators, mesalamine
- Disease activity/remission status
- Crohn’s vs ulcerative colitis
- GI surgery history
- Bowel prep/colonoscopy timing

## Claim to check: “Qiagen PowerSoil kit”

> “DNA was extracted using the Qiagen PowerSoil kit…”

The exact kit name/version matters. Qiagen has multiple related kits, including DNeasy PowerSoil Kit, DNeasy PowerSoil Pro Kit, and older MoBio PowerSoil kits. These have different bead-beating chemistries and can yield different bacterial profiles.

**Recommended fix:** Provide exact kit name, catalog number, manufacturer, and extraction protocol modifications.

Also missing:

- Bead-beating instrument and settings
- Input stool mass
- Extraction batch information
- Randomization of case/control samples across extraction batches
- Negative extraction controls
- Mock community controls
- DNA quantification method

Without these, batch effects and contamination cannot be evaluated.

## Claim to check: “following manufacturer instructions”

> “...following manufacturer instructions.”

This is inadequate because stool DNA extraction usually involves critical choices not fully captured by manufacturer instructions, especially bead beating. Different bead-beating durations and intensities affect recovery of Gram-positive organisms.

**Recommended fix:** Specify mechanical lysis conditions, sample mass, elution volume, DNA QC thresholds, and whether modifications were made.

---

## Claim to check: “V4 region primers 515F and 806R”

> “16S rRNA gene sequencing was performed using the V4 region primers 515F and 806R…”

This is incomplete. There are multiple versions of 515F/806R, including updated primers designed to reduce bias against certain taxa, including archaea and SAR11 in environmental contexts. The exact sequences should be provided.

Also missing:

- Primer sequences
- Barcoding/indexing strategy
- PCR cycle number
- Polymerase used
- Annealing temperature
- Amplicon length
- Number of PCR replicates
- Negative PCR controls
- Sequencing read configuration, e.g., 2 × 250 bp
- Target sequencing depth

**Recommended fix:** Include full PCR and sequencing details or cite a detailed protocol while still reporting key parameters.

> “taxonomy was assigned using the Greengenes database.”

Greengenes has not been meaningfully updated since 2013 and is widely considered outdated for current microbiome taxonomic assignment. This is a major weakness, especially for a manuscript submitted now.

A skeptical reviewer would likely object strongly unless there is a specific reason for using Greengenes, such as comparability with legacy datasets.

**Recommended fix:** Use SILVA, GTDB, or another current database. If Greengenes must be used, justify it and ideally include sensitivity analyses with SILVA.

# 4. Overstated or unsupported claims

## “All statistical analyses were performed using appropriate methods”

> “All statistical analyses were performed using appropriate methods…”

This is not acceptable scientific writing. It asks the reader to trust the authors rather than providing methods. It is also circular: the methods are “appropriate” because the authors say they are.

A reviewer would likely demand full specification of:

- Tests used for alpha diversity comparisons
- Method used for beta diversity group differences
- Whether PERMANOVA was used
- Covariates included
- Whether dispersion was tested, e.g., PERMDISP
- Multiple-comparison correction
- Software and package versions
- Handling of non-normality
- Handling of paired/repeated measures, if applicable
- Effect size reporting

**Recommended fix:** Replace with explicit statistical methods.

Example:

> “Group differences in Shannon diversity were assessed using Wilcoxon rank-sum tests. Beta diversity differences were tested using PERMANOVA with 999 permutations using the vegan package in R, adjusting for age, sex, BMI, and antibiotic exposure. Homogeneity of dispersion was assessed using betadisper. P-values were adjusted using the Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate procedure.”

---

## “significance was set at p < 0.05”

> “...significance was set at p < 0.05.”

This is inadequate and potentially misleading for microbiome data, where multiple comparisons are common. If the study tests multiple alpha metrics, taxa, clinical covariates, or beta diversity distances, unadjusted p < 0.05 inflates false positives.

**Recommended fix:** Specify when nominal p-values are used versus FDR-adjusted q-values. For taxa-level analyses, use FDR correction.

---

## “Alpha diversity was calculated using Shannon index and beta diversity was calculated using Bray-Curtis distances”

> “Alpha diversity was calculated using Shannon index and beta diversity was calculated using Bray-Curtis distances.”

This is incomplete. Shannon and Bray-Curtis require decisions about normalization/rarefaction and feature table construction. Bray-Curtis is sensitive to sequencing depth and data transformation.

Missing:

- Were data rarefied before alpha/beta diversity?
- What rarefaction depth?
- Were counts transformed?
- Were ASVs or OTUs used?
- Were samples with insufficient depth excluded?
- Why only Shannon and Bray-Curtis?
- Were phylogenetic metrics considered, e.g., Faith’s PD, UniFrac?

**Recommended fix:** State normalization procedure and justify diversity metrics.

   The phrase “appropriate methods” is not a methods section. The authors need to specify tests, covariates, permutation procedures, multiple-testing correction, and software.

8. **The analysis may conflate location and dispersion effects.**
