## Overall assessment

This Methods paragraph is too vague to be reproducible and would likely be considered inadequate for a microbiome study, especially one comparing inflammatory bowel disease patients with healthy controls. The biggest problems are not stylistic; they are methodological. Key pre-analytical, sequencing, bioinformatic, and statistical details are missing, and several statements are either unverifiable or overly generic.

# 1. Logical weaknesses

## 1.1 “Standard protocols” is not a method

> “Stool samples were collected from all participants using standard protocols.”

This is logically weak because it asks the reader to accept that collection was appropriate without specifying what was actually done. “Standard protocols” is not self-defining. Different stool collection protocols produce different microbiome profiles depending on collection device, stabilizer, time to freezing, storage temperature, oxygen exposure, aliquoting, and freeze-thaw history.

**Why this matters:**  
In a case-control IBD study, differences between groups could reflect collection artifacts rather than disease biology. For example, if healthy controls collected samples at home and patients provided samples in clinic, that is a major confounder.

**Recommended fix:**  
Specify:

- Home or clinic collection
- Collection kit/device used
- Whether stabilizing buffer was used
- Time from defecation to freezing
- Temperature before freezing
- Aliquoting procedure
- Whether samples were homogenized
- Number of freeze-thaw cycles
- Whether controls and IBD samples were handled identically

---

## 1.2 “Immediately frozen at -80°C” is questionable without operational detail

> “Samples were immediately frozen at -80°C…”

This sounds too clean and may be unrealistic unless all participants defecated in a facility with immediate access to a -80°C freezer. If samples were collected at home, “immediately” is almost certainly false or at least needs qualification.

**Why this matters:**  
Delay to freezing can alter microbial composition, especially for oxygen-sensitive taxa. If IBD patients and controls differed in collection logistics, this could bias results.

**Recommended fix:**  
Report median and range of time from collection to freezing, interim storage conditions, and whether these differed by group.

Example:

> “Participants collected stool at home using OMNIgene-GUT kits and shipped samples at ambient temperature within 24 h. Upon receipt, samples were aliquoted and stored at -80°C.”

or

> “Fresh stool was collected in clinic and frozen at -80°C within 30 minutes.”

---

## 1.3 The paragraph assumes sequencing results reflect biology, but does not address confounding

The text implies that stool microbiome differences can be attributed to health status/IBD, but no design features are described to separate biological differences from confounders.

Missing confounders include:

- Antibiotic use
- Probiotics/prebiotics
- Diet
- Bowel preparation
- Colonoscopy timing
- Corticosteroids
- Immunosuppressants
- Biologics
- 5-ASA use
- Disease activity
- IBD subtype: Crohn’s disease vs ulcerative colitis
- Disease location
- Recent hospitalization
- Age, sex, BMI
- Smoking
- Stool consistency/diarrhea
- Collection site and batch

**Recommended fix:**  
State inclusion/exclusion criteria and variables included in adjusted analyses.

> “All statistical analyses were performed using appropriate methods…”

This is not a method. It is a conclusion about the suitability of unspecified methods. A reviewer cannot evaluate or reproduce the analysis.

**Recommended fix:**

# 2. Factual concerns and claims that should be checked

## 2.1 “All participants” needs verification

> “Stool samples were collected from all participants…”

This should be checked against the actual sample availability. Were all 60 samples successfully collected, extracted, sequenced, and retained after quality filtering? In microbiome studies, it is common to lose samples due to low DNA yield, poor sequencing depth, contamination, or metadata issues.

**Concern:**  
This wording may obscure attrition.

**Recommended fix:**  
Report the number collected, extracted, sequenced, and included in final analysis.

Example:

> “Stool samples were obtained from 60 participants. After exclusion of 3 samples with fewer than 5,000 reads, 57 samples were included in downstream analyses.”

---

## 2.2 “Qiagen PowerSoil kit” may be imprecise or outdated

> “DNA was extracted using the Qiagen PowerSoil kit…”

The exact kit version matters. “PowerSoil” may refer to the older Mo Bio PowerSoil DNA Isolation Kit, Qiagen DNeasy PowerSoil Kit, or DNeasy PowerSoil Pro Kit. These are not identical.

**Why this matters:**  
Extraction kits introduce measurable biases, especially in Gram-positive organisms and low-biomass samples.

**Recommended fix:**  
Provide exact kit name, manufacturer, catalog number, protocol modifications, bead-beating settings, input mass, and elution volume.

---

## 2.3 “Following manufacturer instructions” is insufficient

> “…following manufacturer instructions.”

This is often inadequate because stool extraction commonly involves modifications such as bead-beating duration, heating, inhibitor removal, or altered input mass. Manufacturer instructions alone do not indicate whether samples were homogenized or whether extraction was randomized by group.

**Recommended fix:**  
State:

- Stool mass extracted
- Homogenization method
- Bead-beating instrument, speed, and duration
- Whether extraction batches were randomized
- Whether extraction blanks were included
- DNA quantification method

---

## 2.4 Primer details are incomplete

> “16S rRNA gene sequencing was performed using the V4 region primers 515F and 806R…”

This is under-specified. There are multiple 515F/806R variants, including original and updated versions designed to reduce taxonomic bias. The exact sequences should be given. Barcodes/adapters and amplification conditions are also absent.

**Recommended fix:**  
Include:

- Exact primer sequences
- Primer variants used
- PCR cycling conditions
- Polymerase
- Number of PCR cycles
- Template DNA input
- Replicate PCRs or not
- Negative controls
- Mock community controls
- Amplicon cleanup and quantification method

---

## 2.5 Sequencing platform description is incomplete

> “…on an Illumina MiSeq platform.”

This does not specify read length, chemistry, target depth, multiplexing strategy, or run quality.

**Recommended fix:**  
Report:

- MiSeq kit version
- Paired-end read length, e.g., 2 × 250 bp
- Sequencing facility
- Number of runs
- Whether cases and controls were balanced across runs
- Median reads per sample
- Range of reads per sample
- Quality filtering thresholds

---

## 2.6 “QIIME2 with default parameters” is a major reproducibility problem

> “Sequences were processed using QIIME2 with default parameters…”

QIIME2 is a platform, not a single pipeline. “Default parameters” are not stable across versions and do not specify whether DADA2, Deblur, closed-reference OTU picking, open-reference OTU picking, or another method was used.

**Major concern:**  
This makes the analysis non-reproducible.

**Recommended fix:**  
Specify:

- QIIME2 version
- Denoising method, e.g., DADA2 or Deblur
- Trimming/truncation positions
- Quality-score thresholds
- Chimera removal method
- Minimum feature abundance/prevalence filters
- Whether ASVs or OTUs were used
- Rarefaction depth, if applied
- How samples with low reads were handled

---

## 2.7 Greengenes is outdated

> “…taxonomy was assigned using the Greengenes database.”

Greengenes has not been updated since 2013. It is still used in older studies but is generally considered outdated compared with SILVA or GTDB-based resources. For a current manuscript, reviewers may question why Greengenes was chosen.

**Recommended fix:**  
Justify the choice or use a more current database such as SILVA. If Greengenes is retained for comparability, state the version and rationale.

Example:

> “Taxonomy was assigned using a naive Bayes classifier trained on the SILVA 138.1 database trimmed to the 515F/806R V4 region.”

---

## 2.8 Alpha and beta diversity descriptions are incomplete

> “Alpha diversity was calculated using Shannon index and beta diversity was calculated using Bray-Curtis distances.”

This describes metrics but not statistical testing. Also, Bray-Curtis is a dissimilarity metric, not “beta diversity” by itself. The text does not say how group differences were tested.

**Recommended fix:**  
Specify:

- Rarefaction or normalization approach
- Shannon diversity test, e.g., Wilcoxon rank-sum, linear model
- Beta diversity test, e.g., PERMANOVA
- Covariates included
- Dispersion testing, e.g., PERMDISP
- Multiple-testing correction

---

## 2.9 “Significance was set at p < 0.05” is inadequate for microbiome data

> “…significance was set at p < 0.05.”

Microbiome studies involve many taxa and multiple comparisons. A simple p < 0.05 threshold risks inflated false positives.

**Recommended fix:**  
Use false discovery rate correction for taxonomic comparisons and specify adjusted p-value/q-value threshold.

Example:

> “For differential abundance analyses, Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate correction was applied, with q < 0.05 considered significant.”
