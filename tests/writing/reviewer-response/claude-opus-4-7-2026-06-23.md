# Response to Reviewer Comment

We thank the reviewer for this thoughtful and important critique regarding the rigor of our statistical approach and the interpretation of correlation strength. We agree that transparency around multiple testing and careful discussion of effect sizes are essential for studies of this kind, and we have made several revisions to address both concerns.

**Regarding multiple testing transparency:**

The reviewer is correct that we did not provide sufficient detail about the scope of our testing procedure. To clarify: we performed pairwise Spearman correlations between 77 taxa (filtered for prevalence >10% across samples) and 11 metabolites, yielding a total of 847 individual tests. P-values were adjusted using the Benjamini-Hochberg procedure to control the false discovery rate at q < 0.05. We have now made this fully explicit in the revised Methods section.

Revised text in Methods (Section 2.5, page 8, lines 215–221):

> "Associations between microbial taxa and metabolite abundances were assessed using Spearman rank correlations. Taxa present in fewer than 10% of samples were excluded prior to testing, leaving 77 taxa and 11 metabolites for analysis, resulting in 847 pairwise comparisons. To control for multiple testing, p-values were adjusted using the Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate (FDR) procedure, with significance defined as q < 0.05. Adjusted p-values and the raw p-value threshold corresponding to the FDR cutoff are reported in Supplementary Table S3."

**Regarding weak correlations (r < 0.3):**

We agree with the reviewer that correlations of modest magnitude warrant careful interpretation, even when statistically significant after FDR correction. We have therefore added explicit caveats to the Discussion rather than removing these associations, because we believe several of them remain informative in the context of broader network-level patterns (e.g., taxa that show consistent, modest associations with multiple metabolites in the same pathway).

New text added to Discussion (Section 4.3, page 19, lines 478–486):

> "Several of the associations reported here, while statistically significant after FDR correction, are of modest magnitude (|r| < 0.3) and should be interpreted with appropriate caution. Weak correlations may reflect indirect or context-dependent relationships, inter-individual heterogeneity, or technical variability inherent to compositional microbiome and metabolomic data. We highlight such associations primarily where they form part of coherent patterns—for example, when a single taxon is consistently associated with multiple metabolites in a shared pathway—rather than as evidence of strong individual taxon–metabolite links. Validation in independent cohorts and mechanistic follow-up will be required to establish biological relevance."

We have also flagged correlations with |r| < 0.3 in Figure 4 with a distinct visual marker so that readers can immediately distinguish stronger from weaker associations.

We thank the reviewer for prompting these clarifications, which we believe have meaningfully improved the rigor and transparency of the manuscript.
