To summarize the reviewer’s concerns clearly: (1) the original manuscript did not transparently report the total number of multiple comparisons performed, the effective FDR correction threshold, or full context of the multiple testing workflow, despite noting that FDR correction was applied; and (2) the manuscript did not adequately discuss the biological relevance of statistically significant correlations with r-values below 0.3, which may have limited standalone biological meaning despite statistical significance.

We considered associations significant at a q-value threshold of <0.05, corresponding to an effective raw p-value threshold of ~0.00059 after correction."
   This addition fully clarifies the scope of our multiple testing, the correction method used, and the effective significance threshold, directly addressing the reviewer’s request for transparency.

2. **Interpretation of weak correlations (r < 0.3)**
   To address the second concern, we have added explicit discussion of the limitations and contextual relevance of weak correlations to the manuscript. First, in the Discussion section (page 7, lines 22–25), we now state:
   > "We note that several significant associations identified in this study have Pearson correlation coefficients (r) below 0.3. While these correlations are statistically significant after FDR correction, we acknowledge that their effect size is small, and they should be interpreted with caution as standalone biological findings. However, these weak associations may still provide meaningful insights when integrated into broader microbial-metabolite interaction networks, as they may represent secondary or context-dependent relationships that are not detectable in isolation."
   We fully agree that correlations with r < 0.3 should not be interpreted as strong, standalone biological relationships, and we have added this explicit caveat to prevent overinterpretation. We hope these revisions address the reviewer’s concerns. If the reviewer has additional suggestions for how to further contextualize these findings, we are happy to incorporate them.
