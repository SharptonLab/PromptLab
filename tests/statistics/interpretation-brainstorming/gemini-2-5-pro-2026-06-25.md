### 3. Potential Confounds
These are factors that could have unintentionally biased your results.

*   **Donor Variability:** You likely pooled feces from several lean donors. What if one of them was a "super donor" responsible for the entire effect? The effect might not be generalizable to "lean-ness" but specific to that one donor's unique microbiome.
*   **Cage Effects:** Mice in the same cage share microbiota through coprophagy. If randomization wasn't perfect or if one cage in the FMT-lean group had a particularly strong response, it could skew the group average. Your statistical model should ideally account for cage as a variable.
*   **Food/Water Intake:** Did you measure it? Even a small, non-significant decrease in food intake in the FMT-lean group could contribute to lower glucose over 8 weeks. Pair-feeding is the gold standard to control for this.
*   **Gavage Stress:** The gavage procedure itself is a stressor, which can raise cortisol and affect glucose. While all groups received gavage (except a potential no-gavage control), it's a background variable that could interact with the treatments.
