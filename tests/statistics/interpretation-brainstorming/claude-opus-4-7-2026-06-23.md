**B. Diversity itself is the active ingredient, not specific taxa**
- *Prediction*: Glucose improvement correlates with alpha diversity regardless of community composition
- *Distinguish*: Regression of glucose on diversity metrics vs. specific taxa abundances; this is testable in your existing data

**C. Bile acid or short-chain fatty acid mediator effect**
- *Prediction*: Glucose changes correlate with metabolite shifts, not directly with bacterial composition
- *Distinguish*: Measure cecal/serum SCFAs and bile acid profiles

**D. Reduction of pathobionts rather than addition of beneficial taxa**
- *Prediction*: The benefit comes from competitive exclusion of glucose-dysregulating obese-associated taxa
- *Distinguish*: Selective depletion experiments; correlation of glucose with decreased (not just increased) taxa

**E. Donor variability artifact**
- *Prediction*: If you pooled donors, results reflect pool composition; if you used individual donors, donor identity may drive effects
- *Distinguish*: Report donor-level variance; ideally use multiple independent donors

**F. Inflammation modulation, with glucose as downstream readout**
- *Prediction*: Systemic/gut inflammation markers should change; glucose improvement is secondary to reduced inflammation
- *Distinguish*: Measure inflammatory markers, gut barrier function (LPS, zonulin)

- **Caging effects/coprophagia**: Were groups co-housed or separately housed? Mice eat each other's feces, which can homogenize microbiomes within cages and create pseudoreplication
- **Antibiotic pretreatment**: If used to "clear" the recipient microbiome, residual effects could differ between groups
- **FMT vehicle/preparation differences**: Were lean and obese donor preps processed identically, with equivalent bacterial loads?
- **Stress from gavage**: Did all groups receive equivalent handling, including PBS controls?
- **Food intake**: Was food consumption measured? Microbiota can alter feeding behavior
- **Circadian timing of glucose measurement**: Microbiota influences circadian rhythms
- **Single fasting glucose vs. glucose tolerance**: A point measurement is noisier than a GTT

- Single metabolic readout (fasting glucose); no insulin sensitivity, GTT, or ITT data described
- No body composition (fat mass could change without weight change)
- Modest sample size; secondary comparisons underpowered
- Mouse model—translational caveats to human obesity
- Donor characterization beyond "lean" not specified (diet, age, genetics?)
- Mechanism not established—correlational link between taxa and glucose
- No longitudinal microbiome data to assess engraftment stability
- Unclear whether effect persists after FMT cessation

## 5. Questions for Follow-up

- **Metabolomics**: SCFAs, bile acids, branched-chain amino acids
- **Tissue-level**: Liver and adipose insulin signaling, hepatic glucose production
- **Durability**: Does the effect persist 4-8 weeks post-FMT?
- **Causal taxa**: Mono-colonization studies in gnotobiotic mice with candidate species
- **Reverse experiment**: Does FMT from your treated mice to naïve obese mice transfer the phenotype?
- **Dose-response**: Different FMT frequencies or doses

- ❌ Claims about durability or mechanism
