# Critical Review: EAE + High-Fiber Diet Experiment

## 1. Strengths

- **2x2 factorial design** (EAE × diet) is appropriate and allows you to distinguish diet effects in healthy vs. diseased states
- **Blinded clinical scoring** — essential for EAE since scoring is subjective
- **Longitudinal fecal sampling** captures microbiome dynamics, not just endpoint
- **Multiple readouts** (clinical, microbiome, immune) enable mechanistic linkage
- **Single sex/age/strain** reduces baseline variability
- **You've already identified cage effects** — many researchers miss this

## 2. Missing Controls

**Essential:**
- **Diet acclimation period before EAE induction**: When does the high-fiber diet start? If started at weaning but EAE induced later, you have weeks of differential microbiome shaping. If started at EAE induction, you may not see microbiome effects in time. *This needs to be specified and justified.*
- **Isocaloric/macronutrient-matched diets**: Is the "high-fiber" diet matched for calories, protein, fat, and micronutrients? If you're comparing chow to a purified diet, you have a confound far larger than fiber. You need a low-fiber control diet from the same manufacturer with identical formulation except fiber content.
- **Fiber type specification**: "High fiber" is ambiguous. Soluble (inulin, pectin) vs. insoluble (cellulose) vs. fermentable mixtures have radically different effects. State the exact fiber source and amount.
- **CFA/PT-only sham group**: EAE induction uses Complete Freund's Adjuvant and pertussis toxin, both of which affect immunity and microbiome. Without CFA/PT-only controls, you cannot separate MOG-specific effects from adjuvant effects.

**Nice-to-have:**
- Naive (no injection at all) controls in addition to CFA/PT sham
- Pre-treatment baseline immune measurements

## 3. Potential Confounds

- **Caloric intake**: High-fiber diets often reduce caloric density; mice may eat more or less. Body weight differences alone affect EAE severity.
- **Diet palatability**: Differential consumption → differential nutrition unrelated to fiber.
- **Coprophagy across cages**: Mice eat feces. If cages are in the same rack with shared airflow/handler, you have cross-contamination of microbiomes.
- **Vendor/shipping microbiome**: Were mice from the same vendor, same shipment, same room? Jackson vs. Charles River mice have different baseline microbiomes that dramatically affect EAE.
- **Bedding, water, autoclaving**: All shape the microbiome.
- **Estrous cycle**: Female mice cycle; EAE severity varies with cycle stage. You don't mention synchronization or sampling consideration.
- **Handler effects**: Stress affects EAE. Who handles which cages?

## 4. Threats to Validity

**Internal validity:**
- **Cage as the unit of analysis problem (critical)**: With only 2 cages/group, cage effects are confounded with treatment. If one cage has an outlier microbiome bloom, it looks like a treatment effect. For microbiome studies, **cage is arguably the experimental unit, not mouse** — meaning your effective n for microbiome could be 2, not 12.
- **Randomization at weaning**: Good, but were littermates distributed across groups? If group 1 = litters A,B and group 2 = litters C,D, litter effects are confounded.
- **Blinding gaps**: Who handles the mice daily? Who collects fecal samples? Who runs flow cytometry? Diet groups may be visually obvious (different chow color/smell).

**External validity:**
- Female-only limits generalization (sex differences in EAE and microbiome are large)
- C57BL/6 + MOG-EAE is a specific model; translation to human MS is limited
- SPF mouse microbiomes don't recapitulate human microbiome complexity

## 5. Statistical Concerns

- **"Adequate power at n=10" needs scrutiny**: Power for what effect size? Clinical score differences? Microbiome beta-diversity? Specific cell populations? A single n justification can't cover all your endpoints. Microbiome studies typically need larger n due to high inter-individual variability.
- **Repeated measures ANOVA assumptions**: EAE clinical scores are ordinal, non-normal, often zero-inflated early, and have ceiling effects. Consider mixed-effects models with appropriate link function, or non-parametric approaches (e.g., area under disease curve, day of onset, peak score as separate endpoints).
- **PERMANOVA with cage confounding**: You must include cage as a stratum/blocking factor or your p-values are anti-conservative.
- **Multiple testing**: 16S generates thousands of comparisons. State your FDR correction approach.
- **Correlation analyses**: "Correlate microbiome with immune parameters" — this is exploratory and high-dimensional. Pre-specify which taxa/pathways and which immune endpoints, or treat as hypothesis-generating only.
- **Mortality/dropout**: EAE causes deaths and humane endpoints. How will you handle missing data at day 21?

## 6. Practical Issues

- **EAE variability**: Even in skilled hands, EAE incidence/severity varies between cohorts. Consider running as one cohort or including cohort as a covariate.
- **Fecal sample logistics**: Day 0, 7, 14, 21 — day 21 is peak disease; sick mice may not produce samples readily. Plan for missing samples.
- **Flow cytometry batch effects**: All day 21 samples in one batch ideally, with randomized acquisition order.
- **Diet storage**: High-fiber diets can spoil; fiber sources can vary by lot.
- **IACUC humane endpoints**: Mice reaching score 4-5 must be euthanized, creating informative dropout.

## 7. Prioritized Recommendations

**Essential (fix before starting):**
1. **Use matched purified diets** differing only in fiber content; specify fiber type and amount
2. **Increase cages per group to ≥4**, ideally 6, so cage isn't confounded with treatment; analyze with cage as random effect
3. **Add CFA/PT sham group** to separate adjuvant from MOG effects
4. **Specify and justify diet timing** relative to EAE induction; consider a pre-EAE diet acclimation period (2-4 weeks)
5. **Co-house or systematically mix bedding** across treatment groups before randomization to equalize starting microbiomes, OR document that you accept baseline cage microbiome differences and analyze accordingly
6. **Redo power calculation** for each primary endpoint, especially microbiome
7. **Pre-register analysis plan** distinguishing primary, secondary, and exploratory analyses

**Strongly recommended:**
8. Distribute littermates across all groups
9. Use mixed-effects model (not RM-ANOVA) for clinical scores; pre-specify summary metrics (AUC, peak, onset)
10. Include both male and female mice if feasible, or acknowledge limitation explicitly
11. Plan for dropout: define how mice reaching humane endpoint are handled in analysis

**Nice-to-have:**
12. Add SCFA measurement in feces/serum (mechanistic link between fiber and immunity)
13. Baseline immune phenotyping before EAE induction
14. Second EAE cohort to test reproducibility

## Questions I Need Answered to Refine This Review

1. What is the exact composition of both diets, and from what vendor?
2. When does diet intervention start relative to EAE induction?
3. Are mice from the same vendor and shipment? Co-housed before randomization?
4. What is your primary endpoint — clinical score AUC? Microbiome composition? A specific T cell subset?
5. What effect size did your power calculation assume, and for which endpoint?
6. Who performs each task (handling, scoring, sample collection, flow)?

Address the essentials before you start — these are the kinds of issues that, post hoc, render data uninterpretable rather than just suboptimal.
