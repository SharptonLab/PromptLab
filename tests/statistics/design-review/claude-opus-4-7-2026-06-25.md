# Critical Review of EAE High-Fiber Diet Experiment

## 1. Strengths

- **Factorial 2x2 structure** (disease × diet) lets you distinguish diet effects in healthy vs. diseased states—better than many published studies that only compare EAE+treatment vs. EAE+control
- **Blinded clinical scoring** is essential for EAE (scoring is subjective) and you have it
- **Longitudinal microbiome sampling** at multiple timepoints lets you track dynamics rather than just endpoint snapshots
- **You're acknowledging cage and litter effects upfront**—most papers ignore these entirely
- **Female C57BL/6 mice are the standard, well-validated EAE model**

## 2. Missing Controls

**Critical:**
- **Diet acclimation period before EAE induction.** When did the high-fiber diet start? If at weaning (~3 weeks) and EAE at 8 weeks, that's 5 weeks—reasonable. But if diet starts at EAE induction, microbiome effects won't have stabilized. State explicitly when diets begin.
- **Iso-caloric, matched diet formulation.** "High-fiber" vs. "standard" chow often differ in protein, fat, micronutrients, and caloric density—not just fiber. You need a *purified* diet where only fiber content differs (e.g., AIN-93G base with cellulose vs. inulin/resistant starch). Standard chow vs. purified diet is the most common confound in this literature.
- **What type of fiber?** Inulin, cellulose, psyllium, resistant starch, and pectin have radically different microbiome effects. Specify and justify.

**Important:**
- **CFA + pertussis toxin control** (CFA/PTx without MOG) to separate adjuvant effects from autoimmunity—not always done but valuable
- **Baseline microbiome characterization before diet assignment** to confirm groups start equivalent

## 3. Potential Confounds Producing the Expected Result Without Mechanism

- **Caloric intake / body weight differences.** High-fiber diets often reduce caloric density; lighter mice can have milder EAE independent of microbiome.
- **Food intake measurement.** You note this but don't describe how. Per-cage intake ≠ per-mouse intake.
- **Gut transit time changes** alter immune cell trafficking and inflammation independent of microbiome composition.
- **Short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) production** is the likely mechanism—if you don't measure SCFAs, you can't distinguish "fiber via microbiome" from "fiber via direct SCFA effects" from "fiber via reduced calories."
- **Vendor microbiome.** If mice come from different shipments or vendors, baseline microbiomes vary enormously. Are all mice from the same shipment?

## 4. Threats to Validity

**Internal validity:**
- **Cage effects are severe in microbiome studies.** With only 2 cages per group, *cage is your unit of replication for microbiome analysis*, not mouse. Mice in a cage share microbiomes via coprophagy. Your effective n for microbiome could be 2 per group, not 12. This is the single biggest issue.
- **Co-housing across groups** would help (or hurt, depending on goal)—but you haven't addressed this.
- **EAE incidence is variable** (typically 70-90%); non-incident mice in the EAE groups will dilute effects unless handled in analysis plan.

**External validity:**
- Female C57BL/6 only → no sex-as-biological-variable; NIH now expects justification
- Single facility microbiome → notoriously non-reproducible across institutions
- MOG-EAE models chronic/relapsing MS-like disease but doesn't capture all autoimmune mechanisms; don't overstate "autoimmune disease" generality

## 5. Statistical Concerns

- **Sample size justification is hand-waved.** "Previous studies showed adequate power at n=10" isn't a power calculation. What effect size? For what outcome? Clinical score effects might need n=10, but microbiome diversity differences and flow cytometry have different variance structures. Run formal power calculations for each primary outcome.
- **Repeated measures ANOVA on clinical scores is problematic.** EAE scores are ordinal (0–5), bounded, often zero-inflated early in disease, and non-normal. Better options: mixed-effects models with appropriate link function, area-under-curve analysis, or non-parametric alternatives (Friedman). Day of disease onset and peak score should also be analyzed.
- **PERMANOVA assumes equal dispersion across groups.** Always pair with PERMDISP to check; otherwise significant PERMANOVA may reflect variance differences, not composition differences.
- **Multiple testing.** Correlating microbiome features with immune parameters across hundreds of taxa requires FDR correction; specify your plan.
- **Cage as random effect** must be in every mixed model. With only 2 cages/group this severely limits power—consider increasing to 3-4 cages per group with fewer mice per cage.
- **Randomization at weaning** is good but you should re-randomize across cages within treatment to break litter-cage confounding if possible.

## 6. Practical Issues

- **Diet palatability/intake differences** in the first week can cause weight loss confounding immune readouts
- **EAE scoring drift** over a 3-week experiment—calibrate the blinded scorer with reference videos
- **16S vs. shotgun metagenomics**—16S limits you to genus-level resolution and can't infer function. For mechanistic claims about SCFA-producing taxa, consider shotgun or at least targeted qPCR of key taxa.
- **Day 21 may miss peak disease.** MOG-EAE typically peaks ~day 15-18 and chronic phase extends beyond. Why day 21 specifically? Consider peak-disease + chronic timepoints.
- **Fecal sample storage**—flash freeze immediately; degradation skews community composition

## 7. Prioritized Recommendations

### Essential (do before starting):
1. **Use matched purified diets differing only in fiber content** (e.g., AIN-93G ± defined fiber type). Specify and justify the fiber.
2. **Increase cage number to ≥4 per group** with 3 mice/cage, or explicitly treat cage as the unit of replication in microbiome analyses. This may require more mice or accepting reduced power.
3. **Run real power calculations** for clinical score, alpha diversity, and key immune readouts. Document them.
4. **Add SCFA measurement** (cecal or fecal) to bridge microbiome → mechanism.
5. **Plan analysis for ordinal/zero-inflated EAE scores**—mixed-effects ordinal model or AUC.
6. **Specify diet start date** with sufficient lead time (≥3-4 weeks) for microbiome stabilization before EAE induction.

### Important (strongly consider):
7. **Verify mice are from single vendor shipment**; characterize baseline microbiome before diet starts.
8. **Measure food intake per cage and body weight weekly**; include as covariates.
9. **Add or move endpoint to peak disease (~day 15-18)** or include both timepoints.
10. **Pre-register analysis plan** including primary vs. secondary outcomes and multiple-testing strategy.

### Nice to have:
11. Shotgun metagenomics instead of (or in addition to) 16S
12. Include male mice in a follow-up or as a second cohort
13. CFA/PTx-only control group
14. Germ-free or antibiotic-depletion follow-up to establish microbiome causality

---

## Questions I'd Need Answered to Sharpen This Review

1. What specific fiber and what diet formulation (chow vs. purified)?
2. When does diet start relative to EAE induction?
3. Are mice from one shipment/vendor?
4. What's your primary outcome—clinical score, a specific immune readout, or microbiome composition? This determines power calculations.
5. Will you co-house treatment groups or keep them separate?
6. Do you have resources for SCFA measurement or shotgun sequencing?

The biggest red flags are **cage-level pseudoreplication in the microbiome analysis** and **the diet matching issue**—address those first.
